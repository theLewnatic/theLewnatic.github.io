Learn more on how we plan to help the poverty striken in India

The ceramic market is already a booming one in India, in fact itâ€™s the 8th biggest in the world. That being said, it is terribly unorganized with a few big players controlling 52% of business and small-medium sized producers controlling the other 48%. Our company will work with the small-medium sized operations, often found in small, impoverished villages, to create a cohesive network of production. Through our partnership, we will be able to expose the smaller operations to a global market while creating new jobs for the other villagers surrounding the production locations.

Additionally, we will be able to hopefully improve the materials and equipment the people are already using while maintaining the individuality of different designs that will represent areas all across India and be available to customers across the globe. All of our initial investments and revenue will be put back into the facilities and infrastructure surrounding the needs of the impoverished so that as Pot for Poverty begins to rise, so will the villages that we are working with.
