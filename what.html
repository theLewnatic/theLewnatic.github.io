Learn more about what it is Pot for Poverty does

The people of India have been using red clay to make jugs for thousands of years, dating back to 2500 BC. Today, red clay is still widely used in the ceramic industry of India because of its unique properties. Because of it is a poor conductor of heat, it keeps the water inside cool despite being exposed to hot temperatures and sunlight. Additionally, red clay releases minerals that have proven health benefits into the water that it is holding.

Our products are jugs and bottles made from nearly the same material that the people of India have been using for generations. While still reaping the same benefits from the red clay, we have a team of materials scientists working on the most durable, temperature resistant ceramic mix possible to ensure long lives from all of our products.
